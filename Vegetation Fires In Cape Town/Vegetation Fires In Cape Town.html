1. Introduction: Wildfire Season

Cape Town is a popular tourist attraction situated at the southernmost tip of South Africa. It is famously known for its beautiful views, great food, and diverse cultures. However, it is also infamous for its bush fires that are often, as stated by Westerncape.gov.za, actuated by accidents caused by people. This tyrannical period is called the Wildfire Season and, once started, is difficult to control. Wildfires are a threat to human life and vegetation. Both have a detrimental effect on the standards of living of people that reside within areas that were affected

Problem Context and Motivation

This report is motivated by the negative impact that wildfires have on civilians that live in Cape Town. Perhaps attempting to understand the indicative nature of the causes may create awareness and alert people on steps they could take to reduce the spread of bush fires.

Research Question

Given that we have information surrounding how and where the wildfires started, which cause contributes the most to a fire outbreak?

Hypothesis

As stated in Frontlinewildfire.com, fires are caused by: heat, fuel and oxygen. Locations in the proximity of where the wildfires start do not generate any significant heat that could cause huge fires. Fuel often requires human intervention to spark a fire. The hypothesis may be formally stated: There is a strong correlation between huge fires beginning and people being careless.

2. Data Description: Fire Log Cape Town Dataset

The dataset is taken from Kaggle, contributed by a data scientist under the pseudonym Obrocka as represented on Kaggle. The data collection spans from January 2009 to September 2017, and the author states that there are approximately five months of records missing.

2.1 Features Of the Data and Thier Description

Below we descibe Every Feature in our dataset and possible values they can take

Incident_category</br> This feature describes where the fire happened, and it is categorized as follows, which are self-explanatory. Note that these are the possible values that this feature can take.

  • Redential fire
  • Transport fire
  • Institutional fire
  • Industy fire
  • Motor vehicle accident
  • Storage Facility
  • Crime
  • Commercial fire
  • Public assembly fire
  • Special incidents - fire services
  • Other

District</br> This feature describes in which District did the fires happen. In the data wrangling process, this feature is divided into three other features. It's divided or encoded using "1 hot encoding".

  • North
  • West
  • East

Description_of_Incident</br> This feature describes what caught fire or what caused the fire. And since there is an endless list of what could have caused a fire. This dataset has approximately unique entries in this feature. Therefore listing all of them wouldn't be a good idea. We, however, list a few:

  • Rubbish Fire
  • Vehicle Fire

  • Etc...

Wind</br> This feature describes the wind conditions at the time the fire incident took place and take one of the following values

  • No Wind
  • Light Breeze
  • Moderate
  • Strong
  • Gale-Force

Details_of_cause</br> This feature goes in greater detail to explain how each incident start. The list here will also be endless but like a few features back we are going to describe a few incidents just to give a feel of the data.

  • MP - Bush Tanker
  • Wt - Water Tanker
  • SU-Skid Unit
  • SH - Street Hydrant
  • PH - Private Hydrant

        return col_name[:pos]
